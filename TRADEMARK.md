## Why this exists

Bleepforge is licensed under copyleft terms (AGPL v3 for the editor) precisely to keep forks open-source — anyone can fork, but the fork must also be open. This trademark notice covers the missing protection: someone could take an AGPL-compliant fork, slap their own name on it, and ship it as if it were the original. The trademark notice says clearly: **fork freely under the license, but use a different name.**
